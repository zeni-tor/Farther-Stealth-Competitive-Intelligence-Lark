## How Lark uses this file

**Three ways a conference creates a signal or outreach opportunity:**

1. **Prospect org is presenting or on a panel** — signals governance
   maturity and sector engagement. Bump to Score-1 minimum; combine
   with other signals for compound scoring.

2. **Conference as signal amplifier** — a prospect with a fresh leadership
   change who is also presenting at a sector conference = high conviction
   reset moment. Compound with SIG-001 or SIG-002 for Score-3.

3. **Conference as outreach timing** — knowing a prospect will be at a
   specific event gives the team a warm, non-cold opener. Flag 6 weeks
   before each event.

**Monitoring question Lark asks:**
Are any HubSpot contacts presenting, attending, or mentioned in connection
with this event — and does that create a move window?

**Queries to run for each conference:**
- `"[conference name]" "[org name]" speaker OR presenter OR panelist [year]`
- `"[conference name]" "[org name]" attending OR registration [year]`
- `site:linkedin.com "[org name]" "[conference name]" [year]`

## Tier C — Regional Conferences

Small nonprofits attend regional conferences, not national ones.
Lark uses a query framework rather than a fixed list.

### Regional conference query framework

When sweeping a contact, run:
```
"[state] nonprofit association" conference 2026
"[state] council of nonprofits" annual conference 2026
"[city] community foundation" conference OR convening 2026
AFP "[chapter name]" conference 2026
```
