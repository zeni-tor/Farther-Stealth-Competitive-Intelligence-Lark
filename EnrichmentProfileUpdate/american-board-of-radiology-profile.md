## What Lark currently knows

[ENRICHMENT RUN · 2026-06-24] What they do: The American Board of Radiology (ABR), founded 1934, certifies diagnostic radiologists, interventional radiologists, radiation oncologists, and medical physicists. It writes and administers the board exams that physicians and physicists in radiology must pass to be recognized as qualified specialists. Revenue comes primarily from exam fees and program services (~85% of revenue). Based in Tucson, AZ. [Confirmed · theabr.org/about · retrieved 2026-06-24]

Capital campaigns: No capital campaign. Largest current programmatic initiative: new Diagnostic Radiology Oral Certifying Exam launching 2028 — major format shift (remote videoconference, one-on-one, seven clinical categories). ABR begins accepting volunteer examiners July 2026. Infrastructure and technology investment implied. [Confirmed · theabr.org/announcements/new-diagnostic-radiology-oral-exam · 2026]

Financial health: Recovered and healthy. Deficit of $1.1M in FY2021 (likely COVID-era exam volume impact) returned to breakeven in FY2022, modest surplus in FY2023 ($475K) and FY2024 ($100K). Assets grew from $43M (FY2022 low) to $51.9M (FY2024) — all-time high. Revenue grew 24% from FY2021 to FY2024. Investment income ~$1.2M annually implies actively managed investment portfolio. [Confirmed · ProPublica EIN 41-0773787 · FY2021–FY2024]
