## What Lark currently knows

[ENRICHMENT RUN · 2026-06-24] What they do: C-Path is a Tucson-based nonprofit founded 2005 as a public-private partnership with the FDA. It acts as a neutral convener — bringing together competing pharma companies, regulators (FDA/EMA), academic researchers, and patient advocates — to collectively solve shared drug development bottlenecks before competitive dynamics kick in. In plain English: it gets competing drug companies to share pre-competitive data and tools so new treatments reach patients faster. It does not develop drugs itself. Notable outcomes include the first tuberculosis drug in 50 years and FDA-endorsed Alzheimer's clinical trial simulation tools. [Confirmed · c-path.org/about · retrieved 2026-06-24]

Capital campaigns: No traditional capital campaign. Revenue model is consortium-based: pharma membership fees, NIH/FDA grants, foundation program funding. New major initiative: "One to Millions" (launched March 2026) — a global multi-stakeholder infrastructure build for individualized gene/RNA therapies. Not a fundraising campaign but represents significant new program commitment. [Confirmed · c-path.org · March 2026]

Financial health: Strong and accelerating. Revenue grew 45% in two years: $24.4M (TY2021) → $31.2M (TY2022) → $35.3M (TY2023). Operating surplus doubled 2022–2023. Total assets grew 44%: $18.5M → $26.7M. Unusual for a program-driven nonprofit — suggests deliberate reserve-building or undistributed surplus. At $26.7M in assets (TY2023), C-Path is approaching the range where institutional investment management becomes relevant. 2023 990 data lags ~18 months; current assets may be approaching $30M+.
