What They Do
ABR is the certifying body for diagnostic radiologists, interventional radiologists, radiation oncologists, and medical physicists. Founded 1934, headquartered in Tucson. It writes and administers the board exams that physicians and physicists in radiology must pass to be recognized as qualified specialists. Revenue comes primarily from exam fees (~85% of revenue) plus investment income. Does not provide patient care — it sets and enforces professional standards.
Confirmed theabr.org/about · retrieved 2026-06-24
Capital Campaigns & Fundraising
No capital campaign. The largest current program initiative is a new Diagnostic Radiology Oral Certifying Exam launching in 2028 — a major format shift from computer-based to remote videoconference, one-on-one exams across seven clinical categories. ABR begins accepting volunteer examiners in July 2026. Technology and infrastructure investment is implied for the 2025–2028 period.
Confirmed theabr.org/announcements/new-diagnostic-radiology-oral-exam · 2026
Financial Health   (IRS 990 · FY2021–FY2024)

ABR is healthy and at an all-time asset high. A ~$1.1M deficit in FY2021 (likely COVID-era exam volume impact) has been fully resolved — the org returned to surplus in FY2023 and FY2024, and total assets grew to $51.9M (FY2024 all-time high) from a $43M low in FY2022. Revenue has grown 24% over three years. Investment income of ~$1.2M annually (5.5% of revenue) confirms an actively managed investment portfolio.

What They Do
C-Path is a Tucson-based nonprofit founded 2005 as a public-private partnership with the FDA. It acts as a neutral convener — bringing together competing drug companies, regulators (FDA, EMA), academic researchers, and patient advocates to collectively solve shared drug development bottlenecks before competitive dynamics apply. It gets competing pharma companies to share pre-competitive data and tools so new treatments reach patients faster. Notable outcomes: first tuberculosis drug in 50 years; FDA-endorsed Alzheimer's clinical trial simulation tools; imaging biomarkers for polycystic kidney disease.
Confirmed c-path.org/about · retrieved 2026-06-24
Capital Campaigns & Fundraising
No traditional capital campaign. Revenue model is consortium-based: pharma membership fees, NIH/FDA grants, foundation program funding. "One to Millions" (launched March 2026) is a major new global initiative for individualized gene/RNA therapies — not a donor campaign but represents a significant new program commitment. Translational Therapeutics Accelerator (TRxA) awarded a record $2.48M in BRIDGe grants in 2025 (20th anniversary year).
Confirmed c-path.org/critical-path-institute-launches-one-to-millions · March 2026
